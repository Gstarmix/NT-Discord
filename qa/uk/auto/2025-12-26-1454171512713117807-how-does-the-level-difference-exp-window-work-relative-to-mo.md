# How does the level-difference EXP window work relative to monster level?

**Q:** There's a table showing level differences for EXP gain, but people describe it in two different (seemingly reversed) ways. Which version is correct?

**A:** The EXP window is defined relative to the monster's level, not the player's: a monster gives full (100%) EXP to players from 6 levels below its level up to 4 levels above its level. For example, a level 70 monster gives full EXP to players from level 64 to level 74. This is why bees (around level 61-62) are commonly farmed starting at character level 54-55. The confusion comes from some tables phrasing the same rule from the player's perspective instead of the monster's, which makes it look reversed even though it describes the same window.
