# When should a mage use Seer instead of Ancient Memory, and is Gravity a good replacement for Seer

**Q:** At C10 with Seer +13 and Ancient Memory (AM) +12, is there any situation where Seer should be used over AM? I'm considering dropping Seer for Gravity since I don't enjoy Seer's single-target, low-AoE playstyle. Is Firestorm ever a better choice than wand specialty powers (SP) like Gravity?

**A:** Mage damage depends heavily on elemental advantage, so Seer never truly replaces AM (or vice versa) against enemies of the same element. However, Firestorm can substitute for any wand SP, including Gravity, when an enemy keeps high resistance even after resdrain: above roughly 90 resistance, elemental damage is largely negated, and Firestorm (a gun SP) is much less affected by high enemy resistance, plus its debuffs give a built-in extra 20 resdrain, giving it an edge over wand SPs in that range. Once resdrain brings enemy resistance down to 80-85, elemental SPs start dealing good damage again, and below 80 resistance, elemental-priority SPs like Gravity become the better pick since damage is already strong and its voke matters more.
